# NAIL Institute — Chapter Governance Framework

## 1. Purpose

This governance framework establishes the rules, processes, and accountability
structures for all NAIL Institute Regional Chapters. It ensures chapters operate
with appropriate autonomy while maintaining alignment with the global NAIL mission.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all NAIL Institute Regional Chapters, their leads, members, and activities.

## 4. Global Chapters Committee (GCC)

### 4.1 Composition
- One elected representative from each active chapter
- Two members appointed by the NAIL Institute Board
- Executive Director serves as non-voting ex-officio member

### 4.2 Responsibilities
- Set global standards for chapter operations
- Review and approve new chapter proposals
- Allocate annual budgets to chapters
- Resolve inter-chapter disputes
- Coordinate cross-chapter initiatives
- Report to the Board quarterly

### 4.3 Meetings
- Monthly virtual meetings (first Wednesday, 16:00 UTC)
- Annual in-person meeting at the NAIL Conference
- Quorum: 50% of voting members + 1

### 4.4 Decision Making
- **Consensus preferred** for all decisions
- **Simple majority vote** when consensus cannot be reached
- **Supermajority (2/3)** required for: governance changes, chapter suspension, budget > $10K

## 5. Chapter Formation

### 5.1 Requirements
- Minimum 3 founding members from the region
- Completed Chapter Charter (using official template)
- Identified Chapter Lead meeting role requirements
- Demonstrated need (no overlapping active chapter in the region)

### 5.2 Approval Process
1. Submit Charter as a PR to `chapters/<region>/charter.md`
2. GCC review within 30 days
3. GCC vote (simple majority)
4. If approved: 90-day probationary period
5. Full chapter status after probation review

### 5.3 Probation
- 90 days from approval
- Must demonstrate: 1+ event, 5+ members, active communication channel
- Reviewed by GCC; may extend probation once (90 more days)

## 6. Chapter Operations

### 6.1 Minimum Requirements (Annual)
- Host 6+ events (meetups, workshops, or other)
- Submit quarterly reports to GCC
- Maintain active communication channels
- Participate in monthly GCC sync calls
- Contribute to at least 1 cross-chapter initiative

### 6.2 Reporting
- **Quarterly reports** due: Apr 15, Jul 15, Oct 15, Jan 15
- Must include: membership count, events held, AVE contributions, highlights, challenges
- Template provided by GCC

### 6.3 Budget
- Annual budget allocated by GCC based on: chapter size, activity level, regional costs
- Chapter Lead approves expenditures up to $500
- Expenditures > $500 require GCC approval
- Financial report due with Q4 quarterly report

## 7. Leadership

### 7.1 Chapter Lead
- **Term**: 2 years, renewable once
- **Selection**: Elected by chapter members (simple majority)
- **Removal**: By GCC supermajority vote for cause, or by chapter members (2/3 vote)

### 7.2 Co-Lead
- **Term**: 1 year, renewable
- **Selection**: Appointed by Chapter Lead, confirmed by chapter members
- **Role**: Deputy, succession planning, specific portfolio

### 7.3 Elections
- Held 30 days before current lead's term expires
- Nominations open for 14 days
- Voting period: 7 days
- Electronic voting, anonymous ballots
- Eligible voters: members active for 3+ months

## 8. Membership

### 8.1 Who Can Join
- Anyone with interest in agentic AI security
- No fees for chapter membership
- Must agree to the NAIL Code of Conduct

### 8.2 Active Member Definition
- Attended 2+ events in the past 12 months, OR
- Made 1+ AVE contribution, OR
- Served in a chapter role

### 8.3 Code of Conduct
- All members bound by the [NAIL Institute Code of Conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- Chapter Lead is first point of contact for CoC issues
- Escalation to GCC for serious or unresolved matters

## 9. Intellectual Property

- All chapter-produced materials are licensed under CC BY 4.0
- AVE cards follow the standard NAIL contribution process
- Chapter branding follows the [Brand Guidelines](../templates/brand_guidelines.md)
- Code contributions follow the standard NAIL CLA

## 10. Dispute Resolution

### 10.1 Within a Chapter
1. Direct resolution between parties
2. Mediation by Chapter Lead
3. Escalation to GCC representative

### 10.2 Between Chapters
1. Direct discussion between Chapter Leads
2. Mediation by GCC Chair
3. GCC vote (binding)

### 10.3 Chapter vs. GCC
1. Discussion with GCC Chair
2. Appeal to NAIL Institute Board (final)

## 11. Chapter Suspension & Dissolution

### 11.1 Grounds for Suspension
- Failure to meet minimum annual requirements
- Repeated Code of Conduct violations
- Financial mismanagement
- Inactivity for 6+ consecutive months

### 11.2 Process
1. GCC issues a written warning with 60-day remediation period
2. If not remediated: GCC supermajority vote to suspend
3. Suspended chapters have 90 days to appeal or remediate
4. If not resolved: dissolution by GCC supermajority vote

### 11.3 Dissolution Effects
- Chapter status reverted to "Inactive"
- Budget returned to GCC pool
- Members welcomed to join neighbouring chapters
- Historical records preserved in `chapters/<region>/archive/`

## 12. Amendments

- Any GCC member may propose amendments
- Discussion period: minimum 14 days
- Approval: GCC supermajority (2/3) vote
- Major amendments also require Board approval
- Effective 30 days after approval
