# Regional Chapter Lead — Role Description

## Title
**Regional Chapter Lead, NAIL Institute**

## Commitment
- **Time**: ~8-10 hours/month
- **Term**: 2 years, renewable once (4 years maximum)
- **Compensation**: Volunteer (travel grants available for NAIL conferences)

## Overview

The Regional Chapter Lead is the primary ambassador and organiser for the NAIL
Institute in their geographic region. They build and sustain a local community
of agentic AI security practitioners, coordinate regional research contributions,
and serve as the liaison between their chapter and the global NAIL Institute.

## Responsibilities

### Community Leadership
- Recruit and onboard new chapter members
- Foster an inclusive, welcoming community environment
- Serve as the public face of NAIL in the region
- Respond to regional membership enquiries within 48 hours

### Event Coordination
- Organise monthly meetups (virtual or in-person)
- Plan quarterly workshops or deep-dive sessions
- Coordinate the chapter's participation in the annual NAIL Conference
- Maintain the regional event calendar

### Research & Contributions
- Encourage and review regional AVE card submissions
- Coordinate local red-teaming exercises and research sprints
- Identify region-specific agentic AI threats and trends
- Facilitate knowledge sharing between regional members

### Governance & Administration
- Conduct chapter elections and maintain the leadership roster
- Submit quarterly reports to the Global Chapters Committee
- Manage the chapter's communication channels (Slack, mailing list)
- Ensure compliance with the Chapter Governance Framework

### Global Coordination
- Attend monthly Global Chapter Leads sync (1hr, virtual)
- Represent the region's interests in global NAIL decisions
- Share regional insights with other chapter leads
- Participate in cross-chapter collaborative projects

## Requirements

### Essential
- **Expertise**: Demonstrated knowledge in AI security, ML safety, or cybersecurity
- **Community**: Existing network in the region's AI/security community
- **Communication**: Strong written and verbal communication skills
- **Availability**: Can commit 8-10 hours/month consistently

### Desirable
- Prior community leadership experience (meetups, conferences, open source)
- Published research or writing on AI security topics
- Connections with regional universities or research labs
- Multilingual capabilities relevant to the region

## Support Provided

| Support | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Onboarding** | 1:1 onboarding with Global Chapters Committee |
| **Playbook** | Chapter Handbook with operational templates |
| **Budget** | Annual operating budget for events and materials |
| **Travel** | Travel grants for NAIL conferences and cross-chapter visits |
| **Branding** | Regional logo, slide decks, and marketing materials |
| **Platform** | Access to NAIL community platform and tools |
| **Mentorship** | Paired with an experienced chapter lead for first 6 months |

## Application Process

1. **Express Interest**: Email chapters@nailinstitute.org or open a GitHub issue
2. **Application Form**: Complete the Regional Lead application
3. **Interview**: 30-minute call with the Global Chapters Committee
4. **Community Endorsement**: At least 2 endorsements from the regional community
5. **Appointment**: Formal appointment by the NAIL Institute Board

## Performance Review

- **Quarterly check-ins** with Global Chapters Committee
- **Annual review** based on chapter success metrics
- **Peer feedback** from chapter members (anonymous survey)

## Succession Planning

Chapter Leads must:
- Identify a potential successor within their first year
- Train the Co-Lead as a backup for all responsibilities
- Provide 3 months notice before stepping down
- Complete a handover document using the provided template
