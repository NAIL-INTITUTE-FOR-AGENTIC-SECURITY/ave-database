# 🌱 Sustainability Model

Long-term financial sustainability strategy for the NAIL Institute
for Agentic Security — ensuring the AVE database and community remain
independently funded, open-source, and community-governed.

## Mission Statement

> To maintain a self-sustaining, open-source research institute that
> can operate indefinitely without dependence on any single revenue
> source, donor, or corporate sponsor.

## Core Principles

1. **Open Core** — The AVE database and core tools remain free and
   open-source forever (CC BY 4.0 / Apache 2.0)
2. **Diversified Revenue** — No single source > 30% of total revenue
3. **Community First** — Revenue activities must not compromise
   research independence or community trust
4. **Transparency** — Annual financial reports published publicly
5. **Reinvestment** — Minimum 60% of surplus reinvested in research

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|------------|
| Slow API adoption | Revenue shortfall | Medium | Invest in developer experience; extend free tier |
| Competitor database | Market share loss | Low | First-mover advantage; community moat; open-source lock-in prevention |
| Key person dependency | Operational risk | High (Y1) | Document everything; cross-train; advisory board continuity plan |
| Grant rejection | Revenue shortfall | Medium | Maintain 3+ applications; diversify funders |
| Regulatory change | Compliance cost | Medium | Track EU AI Act, NIST closely; adapt standards proactively |
| Economic downturn | Budget cuts by partners | Medium | Maintain free tier; focus on ROI messaging; extend payment terms |
| Community trust loss | Reputation damage | Low | Transparent governance; community council veto; open financials |
