## Adaptable Abstract (150 words)

AI agents are no longer research prototypes — they're handling customer
data, executing financial transactions, and managing critical
infrastructure. But we lack a shared language for their vulnerabilities.

The NAIL Institute's Agentic Vulnerability Enumeration (AVE) standard
changes that. With 50+ documented vulnerabilities across 14 categories,
AVE provides the common vocabulary that security teams, regulators, and
insurers need to assess agentic AI risk.

In this talk, you'll see:
- Live demonstrations of agent-specific attack chains
- The AVE taxonomy and how to use it
- How to score agent risk with AVSS
- Defence strategies that work in production
- How to get your systems NAIL Certified

Whether you're building agents, securing them, or insuring them,
this talk gives you the framework to reason about agentic AI risk.

## Audience Adaptation Guide

### Security Professionals
- Emphasise: attack techniques, MITRE mappings, red team methodology
- Demos: focus on exploitation chains
- Skip: ML internals, training details

### AI/ML Engineers
- Emphasise: framework-specific vulnerabilities, defence implementation
- Demos: code-level exploitation and patching
- Skip: regulatory compliance, insurance

### Executives / Board
- Emphasise: business risk, regulatory requirements, insurance impact
- Demos: high-level impact demonstrations
- Skip: technical exploitation details

### Regulators / Policy
- Emphasise: regulatory mappings, standards alignment, certification
- Demos: compliance gap analysis
- Skip: exploitation techniques
