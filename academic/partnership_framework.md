# Academic Partnership Framework

## Overview

This framework defines how universities and research institutions can formally
partner with the NAIL Institute for mutual benefit in agentic AI security
research and education.

## Partnership Tiers

### Tier 1: Affiliated Institution
- **Commitment**: Acknowledge NAIL in relevant publications
- **Benefits**: Access to AVE data, community resources, event invitations
- **Cost**: Free

### Tier 2: Teaching Partner
- **Commitment**: Adopt at least 1 NAIL course module, provide student feedback
- **Benefits**: Tier 1 + co-branded course materials, guest lecture access, priority
  support for module adoption
- **Cost**: Free

### Tier 3: Research Partner
- **Commitment**: At least 1 active PhD/postdoc project related to NAIL topics,
  contribute 5+ AVE cards/year
- **Benefits**: Tier 2 + dedicated NAIL liaison, compute grants, joint publication
  opportunities, conference speaking slots
- **Cost**: Free

### Tier 4: Strategic Partner
- **Commitment**: Multi-year research programme, faculty secondment or advisory role,
  host regional events
- **Benefits**: Tier 3 + seat on Academic Advisory Board, co-funded research positions,
  named NAIL lab/centre affiliation
- **Cost**: Negotiated (in-kind or financial)

## How to Partner

1. **Express Interest**: Email academic@nailinstitute.org
2. **Scoping Call**: 30 min call with Academic Programme Director
3. **Proposal**: Jointly draft partnership terms (template provided)
4. **Approval**: Academic Programme Committee review (30 days)
5. **MOU Signing**: Formal agreement executed
6. **Launch**: Public announcement and onboarding

## Mutual Obligations

### NAIL Provides
- AVE database access and research data
- Course modules and teaching resources (CC BY 4.0)
- Community and networking access
- Conference opportunities and travel grants
- Compute resources (for Research and Strategic partners)
- Brand co-usage rights per guidelines

### Partner Provides
- Research contributions (papers, tools, AVE cards)
- Student pipeline (ambassadors, PhD candidates)
- Teaching feedback and module improvements
- Event hosting and local community building
- Academic credibility and network access

## Intellectual Property

- Research IP: Remains with the originating institution
- NAIL receives non-exclusive licence for AVE database use
- Joint publications follow standard academic authorship conventions
- Code contributions to NAIL repos follow project's open source licence
- Course materials: CC BY 4.0 (both parties may use and adapt)

## Duration & Review

- **Affiliated / Teaching**: Indefinite (reviewed annually)
- **Research**: 3-year terms (renewable)
- **Strategic**: Negotiated (minimum 3 years)
- Annual review with joint KPI assessment
