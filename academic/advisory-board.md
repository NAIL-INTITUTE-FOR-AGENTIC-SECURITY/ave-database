# Academic Advisory Board

## Purpose

The Academic Advisory Board provides strategic guidance on NAIL Institute research
priorities, educational standards, and academic partnerships. Board members are
distinguished academics with expertise in AI safety, security, and related fields.

## Composition

The board consists of 7-9 members:
- 5-7 tenured/tenure-track faculty members from partner universities
- 1 industry research leader
- 1 early-career researcher (rotating, 1-year term)

## Responsibilities

1. **Research Direction**: Review and prioritize NAIL research topics annually
2. **Quality Assurance**: Review course modules for academic rigour
3. **Standards**: Advise on AVE schema and AVSS scoring methodology
4. **Partnerships**: Facilitate connections with universities and funding bodies
5. **Mentorship**: Mentor PhD Fellows in the research partnership programme
6. **Governance**: Advise the NAIL Board on academic and research matters

## Meetings

- **Quarterly meetings** (2 hours, virtual)
- **Annual in-person meeting** at the NAIL Conference
- **Ad-hoc consultations** as needed

## Terms of Service

- **Standard term**: 3 years, renewable once
- **Early-career seat**: 1 year, non-renewable (rotating)
- **Emeritus status**: Available for outgoing members with significant contributions

## Nomination Process

1. Nominations submitted by: Board members, NAIL leadership, or self-nomination
2. Review by NAIL Executive Committee
3. Board vote (simple majority)
4. Formal invitation extended
5. Announced publicly on NAIL website
