# Student Ambassador Programme

## What Is It?

The NAIL Institute Student Ambassador Programme empowers university students to
champion agentic AI security on their campuses. Ambassadors lead local events,
contribute research, and bridge their academic community with the global NAIL network.

## Programme at a Glance

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Duration** | 1 academic year (renewable) |
| **Commitment** | ~5 hours/month |
| **Compensation** | NAIL swag, conference access, recommendation letter, certificate |
| **Cohort Size** | 20-30 ambassadors globally |
| **Applications** | Open annually (September intake) |

## What Ambassadors Do

### On Campus
- Organize 1 event per semester (workshop, meetup, or lecture)
- Promote NAIL resources in relevant courses
- Recruit fellow students to contribute AVE cards
- Connect professors with the Academic Programme

### In the Community
- Attend monthly Ambassador calls (1hr virtual)
- Contribute to the AVE database (minimum 2 cards/year)
- Write 1 blog post or create 1 educational resource per year
- Participate in the Ambassador Slack channel

### At Conferences
- Present at the annual NAIL Conference (student track)
- Represent NAIL at local/regional AI and security conferences
- Network with researchers, practitioners, and other ambassadors

## Eligibility

- Currently enrolled in undergraduate or master's programme
- Interest in AI security, cybersecurity, or AI safety
- No prior experience required (training provided)
- All countries and universities welcome
- Must commit to ~5 hours/month for the programme year

## Application Process

1. **Online Application** (September each year)
   - Short form: name, university, motivation (300 words), ideas for campus events
2. **Review** by the Ambassador Selection Committee (2 weeks)
3. **Interview** (15 min, virtual) for shortlisted candidates
4. **Selection** announced October 1
5. **Onboarding** week: training sessions, mentor matching, swag delivery

| Red-Teaming Lab Session | Hands-on | 2 hours | Hard |

## Training Modules (Onboarding)

1. **Module 1**: What is Agentic AI Security? (30 min)
   - Overview of the NAIL Institute and AVE database
   - The 13 vulnerability categories
   - Why this matters now

2. **Module 2**: How to Write an AVE Card (45 min)
   - Schema walkthrough (v1 and v2)
   - Example card analysis
   - Hands-on: write your first card

3. **Module 3**: Organising Campus Events (30 min)
   - Event toolkit overview
   - Working with university administration
   - Promotion and outreach tips

## Recognition

### Ambassador of the Year Award
- Announced at the annual NAIL Conference
- Based on: events hosted, AVE contributions, community impact
- Prize: $500 + conference keynote slot + trophy

### Contribution Leaderboard
- Monthly leaderboard on the NAIL website
- Points for: events, AVE cards, blog posts, recruitment

and inclusivity in all activities.
