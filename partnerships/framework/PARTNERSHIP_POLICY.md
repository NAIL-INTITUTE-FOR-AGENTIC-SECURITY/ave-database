# 📜 NAIL Institute — Partnership Policy

> Governance framework for establishing, managing, and reviewing
> international partnerships.

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how the NAIL Institute enters into partnerships
with external organisations to advance the mission of agentic AI
security through the AVE (Agentic Vulnerability Enumeration) standard.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all formal relationships between the NAIL
Institute and:
- National and international standards bodies
- Government agencies and regulators
- Academic and research institutions
- Industry consortia and trade associations
- AI safety organisations and non-profits

## 3. Partnership Principles

### 3.1 Open by Default
All partnership outputs that can be made public SHALL be published
under open licenses (CC-BY-SA-4.0 for documents, Apache-2.0 for code)
unless specific exemptions are agreed.

### 3.2 Mission Alignment
Partners MUST demonstrate commitment to improving AI safety. Partnerships
that would compromise the independence or integrity of the AVE standard
SHALL NOT be entered into.

### 3.3 Reciprocity
Partnerships MUST provide mutual benefit. One-sided data extraction or
brand association without contribution is not permitted.

### 3.4 Transparency
All active partnerships SHALL be listed in the public partner registry.
The existence of partnerships (though not confidential terms) SHALL be
disclosed to the community.

### 3.5 Independence
No single partner SHALL have undue influence over the AVE standard,
governance, or research direction. The Advisory Board retains final
authority on all technical decisions.

## 4. Tier Definitions

### 4.1 Observer Tier
- **Duration**: 1 year, auto-renewable
- **Requirements**: Signed NDA, designated liaison
- **Obligations**: Attend ≥2 quarterly briefings per year
- **Benefits**:
  - Early access to AVE advisories (48h before public)
  - Read-only API access (elevated rate limits)
  - Quarterly threat intelligence briefings
  - Use of "NAIL Observer" designation

### 4.2 Collaborator Tier
- **Duration**: 2 years, renewable
- **Requirements**: Observer for ≥6 months, demonstrated contribution
- **Obligations**:
  - Contribute ≥4 AVE vulnerability reports per year
  - Share anonymised incident data quarterly
  - Participate in ≥1 working group
- **Benefits**:
  - All Observer benefits, plus:
  - Co-branded research publications
  - Data-sharing pipeline access
  - Priority RFC review and comment
  - Speaking slots at NAIL events
  - Use of "NAIL Collaborator" designation

### 4.3 Strategic Partner Tier
- **Duration**: 3 years, renewable
- **Requirements**: Collaborator for ≥1 year, Advisory Board approval
- **Obligations**:
  - Joint research programme with ≥1 deliverable per year
  - Dedicated liaison and quarterly executive meetings
  - Co-fund ≥1 initiative (research, event, or tool)
- **Benefits**:
  - All Collaborator benefits, plus:
  - Non-voting seat on Advisory Board
  - Priority input on RFC development
  - Joint event co-hosting
  - Enterprise API access
  - Logo placement on NAIL website
  - Use of "NAIL Strategic Partner" designation

### 4.4 Treaty Partner Tier
- **Duration**: 5 years, renewable with review
- **Requirements**: Strategic Partner for ≥2 years, formal MOU
- **Obligations**:
  - Formal MOU signed by authorised representatives
  - Joint regulatory engagement and policy alignment
  - Annual partnership summit participation
  - Resource commitment (staff, funding, or infrastructure)
- **Benefits**:
  - All Strategic Partner benefits, plus:
  - Mutual recognition of certification results
  - Joint standards development
  - Policy drafting participation
  - Voting seat on Advisory Board
  - Co-governance of shared initiatives
  - Use of "NAIL Treaty Partner" designation

## 5. Evaluation Process

### 5.1 Initial Assessment
Partnership proposals are evaluated against the criteria in
`evaluation_criteria.yaml`:
- Mission alignment (0–25 points)
- Technical capability (0–25 points)
- Community reputation (0–25 points)
- Resource commitment (0–25 points)

**Minimum score**: 60/100 for Observer, 70 for Collaborator,
80 for Strategic, 90 for Treaty.

### 5.2 Due Diligence
- Background check on organisation's AI safety track record
- Review of existing partnerships and potential conflicts
- Assessment of data handling practices
- Legal review of proposed terms

### 5.3 Approval Authority

| Tier | Approval Authority |
|------|-------------------|
| Observer | Partnerships Lead |
| Collaborator | Partnerships Lead + 1 Board member |
| Strategic | Advisory Board majority vote |
| Treaty | Advisory Board supermajority (5/7) vote |

## 6. Review and Renewal

- **Annual Review**: All partnerships reviewed annually against KPIs
- **Metrics**: Contribution count, engagement level, impact assessment
- **Escalation/Demotion**: Partnerships may be upgraded or downgraded
  based on performance
- **Termination**: Either party may terminate with 90 days written notice
  (30 days for Observer tier)

## 7. Conflict of Interest

- Partners with competing vulnerability databases MUST disclose this
- Board members with partner affiliations MUST recuse from votes
  involving that partner
- Commercial interests SHALL NOT influence AVE vulnerability ratings

## 8. Data Governance

- Shared data is governed by the Data Sharing Agreement template
- Personal data processing requires GDPR/equivalent compliance
- Vulnerability data shared pre-publication is embargoed
- Anonymisation requirements apply to all shared incident data

## 9. Intellectual Property

- AVE standard and database remain under CC-BY-SA-4.0
- Joint research outputs default to CC-BY-SA-4.0 unless agreed otherwise
- Joint code outputs default to Apache-2.0 unless agreed otherwise
- Pre-existing IP remains with the originating party
- No assignment of IP occurs through partnership

## 10. Amendment

This policy may be amended by Advisory Board majority vote.
Amendments take effect 30 days after publication.
